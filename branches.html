I. Definition of Mathematics

A. Individually necessary and jointly sufficient definition of mathematics:

Necessary Conditions:
  • Abstract (independent of physical objects)
  • Rule-governed (follows explicit rules)
  • Deductive (results follow with necessity)
  • Symbolic (uses symbols)
  • Consistent (no contradictions)
Final Definition:

Mathematics is a formal system of abstract, symbolically represented structures governed by explicit rules of deduction that produce necessarily true conclusions under conditions of internal consistency.

Examples that satisfy:
  • Algebra
  • Geometry
  • Number Theory
Examples that do NOT satisfy:
  • Physics
  • Accounting
  • Empirical Statistics
